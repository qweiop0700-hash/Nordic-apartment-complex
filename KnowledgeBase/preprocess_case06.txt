P1:case06(圖面)


Step1｜特徵元素指認
1. 垂直動線：位於平面中央兩側的樓梯間。
2. 線性元素：貫穿建築中央的雙側客房走廊。
3. 機能空間：客房單元、衛浴空間、樓梯前室與中央服務空間。
4. 服務空間：集中配置於中央核心區的儲藏、設備或後勤空間。
5. 端部空間：建築兩端較大面寬的客房單元。


Step2｜空間關係分析


1. 中央核心組織：樓梯間與服務空間集中配置於建築中央，形成共享核心，縮短各客房至垂直動線的距離並提升疏散效率。


2. 雙側走廊串聯：中央走廊沿建築長向延伸，串聯兩側所有客房單元，建立清晰且高效率的水平交通系統。


3. 客房沿廊排列：客房單元以線性群聚方式分布於走廊兩側，使各房間均可直接連接公共動線並獲得外牆採光面。


4. 核心與客房的層級關係：由公共走廊進入客房，再進入衛浴空間，形成「公共－半私密－私密」的空間層級遞進。


5. 衛浴集中配置：客房內衛浴多鄰接走廊側集中設置，藉由設備管線整合降低施工與維護成本。


6. 對稱式平面布局：平面以中央核心為基準向兩側鏡像展開，客房模組重複排列，提高空間使用效率與結構經濟性。


7. 共用動線系統：所有客房共享中央走廊與樓梯系統，透過集中式交通空間減少公共面積占比並提升營運效率。


8. 端部客房強化景觀：建築兩端客房因擁有雙向或較開闊的外部界面，形成相較一般客房更具景觀與採光優勢的空間節點。


9. 疏散節點鄰近配置：樓梯間鄰近主要走廊節點設置，使各客房能以最短路徑連接逃生動線，符合旅館建築安全需求。


10. 公私分區明確：中央走廊作為公共流通空間，客房則退縮於兩側形成私密區域，透過門廳與房門界定公共與私人領域。


P2:case06(室內)


1. 材料
大面積淺色木質天花、木地板與木製家具以溫潤細膩的紋理包覆空間，搭配混凝土柱與黑色金屬構件的剛硬質感，營造兼具自然溫度與結構穩定感的氛圍。


2. 顏色
空間以淺木色、米白色與灰色為主調，透過暖色木材中和冬季窗景的冷色調，使整體環境呈現平靜、舒適且具北歐特色的感受。


3. 設計語彙
連續的木格柵天花與規律排列的吊燈形成強烈線性秩序，引導視線朝向窗外景觀延伸，強化空間的深度感與開放感。


4. 光線
暖色溫吊燈向下投射柔和光暈，搭配大面積窗面引入的均質自然光，形成溫暖人工光與冷調自然光的對比，營造舒適且具層次的室內氛圍。


5. 窗景
落地玻璃完整框取雪地、湖泊與山景，自然光大量滲入室內，使用餐空間與接待空間獲得遼闊視野，提升放鬆感與地域連結性。


6. 整體氛圍
天然木材、規律構件與北歐冬季景觀共同塑造出溫暖而寧靜的空間氣質，在開放透明的環境中展現兼具自然感與精品旅館質感的體驗。


P2:case06(室外)


1. 材料
大面積木構造立面以溫潤自然的木紋降低高層建築的壓迫感，搭配玻璃欄杆與規律開窗，展現兼具自然感與現代性的視覺印象。


2. 顏色
暖色系木材外牆與周圍藍天、白雪及綠地形成冷暖對比，使建築在自然環境中呈現溫暖、親近且鮮明的地標形象。


3. 設計語彙
垂直向上的木構框架、規律窗列與層層退縮陽台共同強化建築的垂直性，引導視線向上延伸，營造挺拔且具標誌性的空間感受。


4. 綠化
大片草地、樹木與周邊山林形成高覆蓋率綠化環境，柔化高層量體尺度並提升空間的休閒感與親自然體驗。


5. 周遭環境
建築座落於開闊的山谷與湖畔聚落環境中，周圍低密度開發、丘陵地景與季節變化明顯的自然景觀，共同營造寧靜且具地域特色的場所氛圍。


6. 整體氛圍
木質高塔在遼闊自然地景中形成鮮明地標，透過自然材料、開放視野與綠色環境的結合，展現永續、溫暖且貼近自然的北歐建築氣質。

Text description provided by the architects. We have all the responsibility to find new ways of thinking. And not only thinking, but actually doing. Mjøstårnet is the first time in history that something like this has been constructed on this scale. It’s also, the first time in history that the materials and the expertise have been sourced locally. Our client says that standing on top of the viewing platform of Mjøstårnet, you can “actually see” where the timber comes from and where it was processed. And that’s part of our sustainable movement being put into practice. Together with our client and constructor, we began to sketch out the project in early 2015. 
Together with Moelven Limtre, Norway’s leading glulam manufacturer and the specialists at Sweco, the engineering company, the team came together, to construct an 18-floor building between 80 to 90 meters high in Brumunddal. The result is the world's tallest timber building according to the Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat. Achieving this required an unparalleled level of skill amongst those who participated, and it is the interaction and cooperation between all the remarkable people that made this record possible. 
We believe Mjøstårnet is an exciting example of a project where you can find new solutions to old questions. Brumunddal is a small city with 10 000 residents, 140km north of Oslo. The site lays on the riverbank of Brumunda, overlooking Lake Mjøsa, Norway larges lake, and its surroundings. The name “Mjøstårnet” is Norwegian and means “The tower of Lake Mjøsa”. The surrounding areas of Brumunddal are famous for its forestry and wood processing industry 
Mjøstårnet is made from about 3 500m3 of timber or about 14 000 threes. Healthy and sustainable forestry is therefore very important if a project like Mjøstårnet and other timber buildings is to succeed. When you are passing by on the highway you only see the building up close for some seconds and in those seconds, you should be convinced that this is a timber building 
Brumunddal is perhaps a place you drive past or through without experiencing the nature and qualities of the place. But now Mjøstårnet puts Brumunddal on the map. The tower is part of a comprehensive area planning, focusing on connecting Brumunddal to the banks of Mjøsa. And from being closed off from the seafront, Brumunddal has now gained a new network of walking and cycling connections between the city center and Mjøstårnet, and the surrounding park facilities. This ensures access to the lake for all users.
Mjøstårnet is a modern mixed-use tower, with facilities that the residents, locals, and visitors can exploit. The tower consists of 18 stories with different programs. The official architectural height verified by the CTBUH is 85,4 meters. Each floor is about 640 m2. The total program for the tower is about 10 500m2 with an additional 4 500m2 public bath. 
The ground floor is public, with lobby, reception and restaurant. The new building is complemented by a public swimming pool with two 25 meters length pools in the low-rise building adjacent to the tower. 
Above the entrance story are building services and conference floors. Five office stories and a four-story hotel with 72 rooms. Thirty-three residential units with balconies overlooking the lake are on floors 12-16 The top two floors are divided into three further residential units, an exhibition room and a public viewing terrace on both the 18th and 19th floor.  
We think it's exciting to see that wood construction has gained a new renaissance and we are proud to be able to help develop wood architecture to new heights. Mjøstårnet is not the blueprint of a tall timber building, but a contributor to further sustainable development. Sustainable wise, the most important aspect of this building is to show that it is possible to build large, complex timber buildings, and in that fashion, inspire others to do the same
