Step2｜空間關係分析
1. 核心集中配置
   * 電梯、樓梯與設備空間集中於平面中央形成核心筒，所有住宅與辦公空間皆圍繞其配置，以縮短動線並提升垂直運輸效率。
2. 放射式單元組織
   * 五戶住宅單元沿核心筒向外放射展開，各戶均直接面向外牆採光面，使所有主要生活空間皆能取得自然採光與景觀視野。
3. 公私層級遞進
   * 空間由公共電梯廳、共享走廊逐步進入住宅玄關與起居空間，形成由公共至私密的層級過渡，提高居住隱私性。
4. 機能集中服務帶
   * 廚房、衛浴與管道空間集中鄰接核心筒配置，將住宅主要起居空間釋放至外圍立面，以提升景觀與採光品質。
5. 異形量體順應平面
   * 不規則外牆輪廓直接塑造不同尺度與格局的住宅單元，使各戶獲得獨特空間形態與多向景觀條件。
6. 住宅與陽台連結
   * 客廳與主要起居空間鄰接外部陽台配置，建立室內外生活延伸關係，同時增加自然通風與戶外活動機會。
7. 辦公空間環繞配置
   * 辦公樓層以核心筒為中心形成開放平面，工作區沿外圍窗帶展開，使大部分辦公座位均能共享採光與城市景觀。
8. 開放辦公與服務核心分離
   * 會議室、茶水間及衛生空間集中於核心附近，外圍保留為彈性辦公區域，以提升空間使用效率與可變性。
9. 垂直機能分區
   * 建築由地下層儲藏與自行車停車空間、低層餐飲商業、中層辦公、高層住宅及屋頂露台所組成，形成明確的都市混合使用層級結構。
10. 辦公與住宅轉換平台
   * 第八層設置露台與共享空間作為辦公與住宅機能的轉換介面，透過戶外公共空間緩衝不同使用族群之間的活動差異。
11. 量體高低對話
   * 剖面呈現高低雙塔量體配置，較高住宅塔與較低辦公量體共同圍塑中央開放空間，以改善日照條件並降低量體壓迫感。
12. 屋頂公共化
   * 兩座量體頂層均規劃露台空間，透過高空公共活動平台提供住戶交流、休憩與觀景機會，提升社群互動性。
13. 景觀導向配置
   * 起居空間、辦公空間與露台皆優先配置於量體外圍及開口較大區域，形成以景觀資源為核心的空間組織策略。
14. 混合使用共生
   * 商業、辦公與住宅共享同一垂直結構與交通系統，但透過樓層分區與獨立出入口進行管理，兼顧使用效率與機能獨立性。
P2:case15(室內)
1. 材料：清水混凝土天花與結構體搭配深色木質飾面及布質家具，透過粗獷與溫潤材質的對比營造兼具理性與舒適的空間感受。
2. 顏色：空間以米白、暖木色與灰色混凝土為基底，輔以綠色燈具、綠色沙發及暖色家具點綴，形成沉穩中帶有活力的氛圍。
3. 設計語彙：斜向牆面、木格柵天花與幾何框架元素強化空間方向性，引導視線朝向窗景與會議核心，提升空間層次與辨識度。
4. 光線：大面開窗引入柔和自然光，搭配線性吊燈、圓形吊燈與間接照明，使空間呈現明亮且均勻的工作與生活氛圍。
5. 窗景：落地窗與轉角窗將城市街景、周邊建築及遠方綠地納入室內視野，增加空間開放感並提供與外部環境的連結。
P2:case15(室外)
1. 材料：立面以垂直木質板材包覆量體，細膩木紋與溫潤質感弱化大型建築尺度，營造親近且具自然氣息的都市意象。
2. 顏色：暖棕色木立面搭配深色窗框與玻璃反射，形成冷暖對比，使建築在城市環境中展現溫暖而穩重的視覺特質。
3. 設計語彙：傾斜量體、錯動窗戶與懸挑陽台打破傳統塔樓的垂直單調性，引導視線向上延伸並強化建築的雕塑感與動態感。
4. 綠化：基地周邊僅配置少量街道樹木與景觀植栽，適度軟化建築邊界並提升都市空間的舒適感。
5. 周遭環境：建築位於現代商業與住宅混合街區之中，與玻璃辦公大樓及低層街廓形成新舊尺度對比，展現鮮明的都市地標性與公共辨識度。

Text description provided by the architects. In the historic industrial area of Nydalen by the river Akerselva in Oslo, Norway, Snøhetta is excited to unveil Vertikal Nydalen, a mixed-use building with a simplified and self-sufficient climate system, and natural ventilation. The building also forms a new town square for the area. 


The site Gullhaug Torg next to Akerselva has been used as a parking lot while Nydalen has undergone its transition from a traditional industrial area to a modern business area over the past decades. As part of the effort to transform the district into a vibrant residential area, the scope was to create a town square and car-free zone along with a high-rise building. 


Vertikal Nydalen has restaurants at street level, offices on the following five floors, and apartments on top. To maintain certain sightlines and gain a lighter impression, the building is divided into two volumes of different heights, where only one part exploits the plot's maximum potential of 18 stories. Various pockets and passageways are created for the public on the ground floor around the building. At the same time, the design ensures good sunlight conditions and minimal wind in the new plaza. 


Triple-zero solution. As a 'triple zero solution' building, no energy is purchased for heating, cooling, or ventilation. This is achieved using geothermal wells, PV panels, a low-exergy system for heating and cooling, and natural ventilation. The project has more than 50 percent overall reduction of CO2 emissions from materials, transport, and energy compared to a reference project. 


Water from geo-wells in the ground circulates in the clay walls and concrete slabs when heating or cooling is needed. The heat absorbed into the concrete walls during the day is released at night and contributes to stable temperatures in the building. The PV panels on the roof power the heat pump that controls the heating and cooling system 


No fans needed. As traditional ventilation systems in office buildings often require a lowered ceiling of up to one meter from the slab, this natural solution without ducts and fans saves a lot of space – space that is better used for light and openness, and that can drastically enhance the spatial qualities of the rooms.
The angled facade is designed to create pressure differences that enable air to move through the building. The air enters through valves in the façade, which open and close as needed. When two windows open on different sides of the building, the pressure difference forces the air to move through the premises, so the air circulates. 
In addition to minimizing the space required for technical elements, the energy use for ventilation, the CO2 footprint in the construction industry, and increasing the spatial qualities of the building – naturally ventilated offices have less need for remodeling and technical maintenance, so the spaces are designed to last and adapt to the clients' changing needs. 
Inside and out. A concrete core ensures a slow release of heat and cold from the floors. Steel constructions support the decks, while the façade is made up of wooden elements clad in heat-treated pine, which creates a warm and tactile expression on the outside. The façade is further characterized by the 43 angled balconies that stand out from the wooden surface with their steel finish. The railings are perforated, while the undersides are multifaceted and capture the light from different angles, providing observant passers-by with various expressions throughout the day. 
The untraditional solutions set certain parameters for the interior. Any adage of materials would lower the effects of the raw concrete and the generous ceiling height on the warming and cooling effect of the building. Therefore, the concrete is unveiled as much as possible throughout, and only a thin oak parquet layer is glued onto the concrete flooring of the apartments. Large windows allow plenty of light, a rare quality for urban new builds, and views over the surrounding areas, with most reaching as far as the Oslo fjord.
